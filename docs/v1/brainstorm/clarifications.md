[5] consultation
- an idea that was inspired by wanting to build stronger customer relationships
- consultation itself is not a core of the business, but stronger customer relationships are
- we were just thinking, customers already get to choose from curated kits, or maybe even to build their own kit, this might help with themeing or even to let pros have freedom to customise, but how about those who are just beginning to step out of their comfort zone and adventure, it would be nice if we can show them that we care just as much as them about their trip (if not more), and we can show this by proactively suggesting what they might need
- in short, we dont want a mechanical experience where someone goes to the kiosk at mcdonalds, select their set, build a meal, and that's it, we want it to be authentic like how certain cafes do it, we want customers leaving with their rentals thinking wow, the people are so friendly, i want to keep renting from them

[6] logistics
- in the future when we have enough budget to set up a physical location, then in-person would be amazing
- without a physical location, we might still be meeting our customers either at an agreed pick up location where we can go through how to use their gear with our customers
- or we might do delivery in person and still be able to engage with our customers
- but it might not be scalable or sustainable, we might eventually need to rely on 3rd party delivery services where they just get a kit without interaction with us
- so in that case maybe we need to rely more on the packaging, we dont just include the kit but have a very nice onboarding flow to help them get started e.g. cheat sheet inside the kit with QRs that can be scanned to learn more about something (our own recorded videos for example), or maybe even a series of emails / whatsapp messages (something like indiahikes where they send 11 emails leading up to trek day to tell you all about how to be prepared, how to make the best of the trekking experience)

[1] scale vs relatinships paradox
- the priority is convenience and seamlessness first, we get the logistics down to a science, then potentially in the future we can incorporate the idea of personal customer relationships - but we should also not neglect that we can try to integrate the customer relationship part early whenever we can and it's feasible
- e.g. when a first timer comes to rent, theyd like to ask questions about their rental, we want to make sure the option to do so is up front and center and effortless

[8] community building

[9] content strategy

[10] geographic expansion
- we start in KL and PJ first because that's where we're based in
- for the rest of malaysia, we can consider other states / locations that adventurers / tourists are likely to go, not necessarily to be in each and every state - this is what i meant by 'cuti-cuti malaysia', but we dont have to use this exact term
- lets not call it phases, phases seem more short term, these are really long term things, id say rest of malaysia is already the moonshot, and leading up to it are the super ambitious milestones
- realistically, i hope to be the top rental service in KL/PJ within 5 years and we double down on it with a solid presence and strong customer base and maybe even physical location
- ideally the dream is that people can rent something, and collect it from the airport at their destination country - saves hassle of pickup/luggage/etc. and they have local support on their equipment rather than having to contact us back in malaysia about it

[3] turnaroudn tiem agreements with customer
- basically we let customers select the dates for actual usage of the rental, then we already include additional dates as part of the package to facilitate pickup/delivery of the item, or when they get back and need time to transfer their footage - ultimately we want them to feel like they're only paying for what they're using, and they're not burning money otherwise
- customers dont negotiate return times, but we can agree on a window for pickup/delivery/dropoff with the customers with clear boundaries and cutoff times
