## A. Market & Customers

**Target Customer Profile (First 1000):**
- Hard requirements: Can afford to travel, want to travel while young
- Desires: Beginning to explore, want unique ways to showcase life, capture hard-to-capture moments, hold high standards
- Outcomes: Creative life documentation (e.g., "I really want a camera for scuba diving")
- Demographics: Young adults, city folk with disposable income, tech-savvy

**Rental Frequency:**
- Vacation renters: 1-3 times per year minimum
- Lifestyle documenters: potentially monthly (music producers, content creators, hobbyists)
- Need market research to determine frequency threshold where buying becomes more economical
- Opportunities: referral deals between customers, loyalty programs (rent enough and get discount to buy)
- Friction point: very regular renters might as well buy their own

**Customer Segments:**
- Primary (50-60%): Casual adventurers - annual/bi-annual travelers wanting professional documentation without commitment
- Secondary (25-35%): Content creators/lifestyle - musicians, artists, small business owners needing equipment for marketing
- Tertiary (10-20%): Serious hobbyists - regular outdoor enthusiasts, may eventually buy but want to trial first

**Why Rent vs Buy:**
- Freedom of choice: try different equipment every time, no commitment
- Financial flexibility: no large upfront investment (action cameras cost RM2000-5000+)
- Chase latest tech: always access newest models without buying and reselling
- Trial before purchase
- Episodic usage: only need gear occasionally
- No maintenance burden: no storage, cleaning, repairs, upgrades (note: we should be educated about insurance and self-maintenance)
- Activity-specific needs: different activities need different gear configurations

**Malaysia Market - Need Further Research:**
- Current rental market landscape and competition
- Malaysian consumer behavior around renting vs buying
- Popular adventure activities and tourism patterns
- Disposable income levels of target demographic
- Cultural attitudes toward renting equipment
- Logistics and delivery infrastructure in major cities
- Insurance and legal framework for rental businesses

**Moat Priority:**
1. Bold identity
2. Seamless UX - website, convenient pickup/dropoff
3. Customer relations and service - community of adventurers
4. Insurance structure - low barrier to entry, allowance for adventure/wear and tear
5. Loyalty deals, unique promotion structures

**Why Pick Us at Similar Price:**
- Identity and authority of Klang Valley, community of adventurers to belong to
- Lowest barrier to entry and easiest process to follow
- Not just one type of rental (action cams) but adventure kits/lifestyle kits
- We care more than customers do about the experience of their vacation

## C. Risk, Loss & Insurance

**Worst-Case Loss Scenario:**
- Lose the core item (e.g., action camera) together with the kit provided
- Most likely: customer disappearing with gear, or gear destroyed/damaged beyond rental use

**Legal Recourse:**
- Might not always be worth pursuing legal avenues even if we have them
- At best a scare tactic

**Dispute Handling:**
- Should always keep documentation/pictures of equipment before and after renting (engineering style)

**Insurance Considerations:**
- Thinking insurance might be viable to achieve lower barrier to entry
- Less deposits and less legal hoop jumping

## D. Unit Economics

**Break-Even Calculation:**
- Average action cam: RM 2-3k
- Market rental price: up to RM 70-80/day for premium latest models, with top-ups for larger SD card, etc.
- For cam renting at RM 60/day: takes about 50 days of rental to recover RM 3000

**Equipment Lifespan:**
- New gear releases at least once a year
- Rental price expected to drop every year

**Deposit Impact:**
- Lower deposit is always higher risk - if customer runs away we lose more money
- Deposit shouldn't affect cash flow too much since we shouldn't touch deposit until rental period is over or item is lost

**Utilization:**
- Seasonal fluctuations are real if purely vacations
- If it's for lifestyle, possibilities open up
