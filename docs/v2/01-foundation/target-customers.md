# Target Customers

## First 1,000 Customers Profile

### Hard Requirements
- Can afford to travel
- Want to travel while they can, make the best of their youth
- Tech-savvy, comfortable with online platforms

### Psychographics
- **Beginning to explore** - stepping outside comfort zone
- **High standards** - don't half-ass things, hold themselves accountable
- **Pride-driven** - want to look back with pride at life-defining moments
- **Creative expression** - want unique, creative ways to showcase their life
- **Capture hard-to-capture moments** - evidence of experiences worth remembering

### Demographics
- Young adults (mid-20s to early 30s)
- Klang Valley city folk
- Disposable income
- Tech-savvy

### Desired Outcomes
- Creative life documentation (e.g., "wow I really want a camera for scuba diving")
- Professional-quality content without professional investment
- Freedom to experiment with different equipment

---

## Customer Segments

### Primary: Casual Adventurers (50-60%)
**Profile:**
- Annual or bi-annual travelers
- 1-3 trips per year minimum
- Want professional documentation without commitment
- Activities: diving, hiking, road trips, travel with experiential angle

**Why they rent:**
- Episodic usage — only need gear occasionally
- Don't want large upfront investment (RM 2,000-5,000+ for action cameras)
- Want to try different equipment each time

---

### Secondary: Content Creators / Lifestyle (25-35%)
**Profile:**
- Musicians, artists, small business owners
- Need equipment for marketing and brand building
- Potentially monthly rental frequency
- Regular documentation of creative projects

**Examples:**
- Music producers capturing live band experiences
- Content creators documenting music videos
- Hobbyists pursuing regular outdoor activities
- Anyone documenting life in general, not just vacations

**Why they rent:**
- ROI-focused — equipment enables revenue
- Need variety for different projects
- Trial before committing to purchase

**Note:** This segment provides more consistent year-round revenue vs. seasonal vacation rentals.

---

### Tertiary: Serious Hobbyists (10-20%)
**Profile:**
- Regular outdoor enthusiasts
- May eventually buy their own equipment
- Want to trial before purchase
- Know what they want

**Why they rent:**
- Testing specific models before buying
- Need specialized gear for specific activities
- Access to latest tech without buying and reselling

**Friction point:** Very frequent renters might reach threshold where buying becomes more economical. Solution: loyalty programs, referral deals, discount-to-buy offers.

---

## Rental Frequency Patterns

| Segment | Frequency | Revenue Pattern |
|---------|-----------|-----------------|
| Vacation renters | 1-3x per year | Seasonal peaks |
| Lifestyle documenters | Monthly potential | Year-round stability |
| Serious hobbyists | Variable | Trial-to-purchase path |

**Key insight:** Not just vacation-focused — also for documenting life in general. Lifestyle segment opens up year-round revenue and reduces seasonal dependency.

## Why Rent vs. Buy?

### Financial Flexibility
- No RM 2,000-5,000+ upfront investment
- Pay only for what you use
- No depreciation concerns

### Freedom of Choice
- Try different equipment every time
- No commitment to one model
- Experiment without financial risk

### Chase Latest Tech
- Always access newest models
- No need to buy and resell when upgrading
- Stay current without capital outlay

### Trial Before Purchase
- Test equipment before committing
- Validate if activity is long-term interest
- Make informed buying decisions

### Zero Maintenance Burden
- No storage requirements
- No cleaning, repairs, or upgrades
- No learning curve for maintenance

**Note:** We should be educated about insurance and self-maintenance to keep costs down and turnaround fast.

### Activity-Specific Needs
- Different activities need different gear configurations
- Diving kit ≠ hiking kit ≠ studio kit
- Renting provides flexibility without owning multiple setups

## Market Validation Data

### Demographics Validation
- **70% of Kuari hikers are mid-20s to early 30s** — validates target age range
- Young Klang Valley professionals with disposable income but limited storage space

### Behavioral Trends
- **"Revenge travel" post-pandemic** — making up for lost time, seeing the world
- **Solo travel trend among young Malaysians** — stepping back from daily demands, reconnecting with personal interests
- **Mindfulness and self-care alignment** — travel as wellness, not just tourism
- Strong social media culture = desire to document experiences professionally

### Market Opportunity
- Existing rental market is fragmented and unprofessional = opportunity for disruption
- Growing adventure tourism in Malaysia (Sipadan diving, Cameron Highlands hiking, etc.)

## Customer Acquisition Threshold

**Question to research:** What's the frequency threshold where buying becomes more economical than renting?

**Potential solutions:**
- Referral deals between customers
- Loyalty programs: rent enough times, get discount to buy
- Partnerships with retailers: refer customers who want to buy, earn affiliate fees
- Deals with other retailers if we refer customers who rent to the point they want to buy

**Balance:** Too frequent renters might as well buy their own — need clear economics on when to encourage purchase vs. continue renting.
