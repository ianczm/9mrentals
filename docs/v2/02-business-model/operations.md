# Operations & Logistics

## Inventory Management System

### Core Requirements

**Real-Time Tracking:**
- Availability by date for each item
- Buffer time between rentals for inspection/maintenance
- Prevent double-bookings
- Handle overlapping bookings and edge cases
- Sync availability across all platforms

**Component-Level Tracking:**
- Track individual items (for customization)
- Track complete kits (for pre-configured packages)
- Ensure components available for both custom and pre-configured orders
- Prevent cherry-picking that leaves incomplete kits

**Customer-Facing:**
- Link to storefront for real-time visibility
- Clear display of what's available when
- Show complete kit contents
- Customization interface for pros

**Approach:** Build custom inventory tracking system, integrate with website.

## Inspection & Maintenance Protocols

### Before Every Rental (Outgoing Inspection)

**Documentation:**
- Photo/video documentation of equipment condition
- Checklist for each item type
- Note any existing wear or marks
- Customer signs off on condition

**Quality Check:**
- Test all equipment functionality
- Verify all kit components present
- Charge batteries fully
- Format memory cards
- Clean and sanitize

**Purpose:** Establish baseline condition, prevent disputes, ensure customer gets working equipment.

---

### After Every Rental (Return Inspection)

**Documentation:**
- Photo/video documentation of returned condition
- Compare to outgoing condition
- Note any new damage or wear
- Assess against "acceptable wear" standards

**Quality Check:**
- Test all equipment functionality
- Verify all kit components returned
- Check for damage beyond acceptable wear
- Identify needed repairs or replacements

**Turnaround Decision:**
- **Quick turnaround:** Minor cleaning, ready for next rental
- **Deep cleaning:** More extensive maintenance needed
- **Repair required:** Remove from inventory until fixed
- **Replacement needed:** Equipment beyond repair or outdated

**Purpose:** Maintain quality standards, assess damage fairly, plan maintenance.

---

### Self-Maintenance Capability

**Why It Matters:**
- Faster turnaround = higher utilization
- Lower costs = better margins
- Less dependency on external services

**We Should Be Educated About:**
- What maintenance we can do ourselves
- What requires professional service
- How this affects warranty or insurance
- Replacement parts inventory needed
- Tools and skills required

**Examples:**
- Cleaning and sanitizing
- Battery maintenance
- Firmware updates
- Minor repairs (mounts, straps, cases)
- When to replace vs. repair

---

## Turnaround Time Management

### Minimum Turnaround Time

**Factors:**
- Inspection time (30 min - 1 hour per kit?)
- Cleaning and sanitization
- Testing functionality
- Charging batteries
- Packaging for next rental

**Buffer in Booking System:**
- Build in minimum turnaround time between rentals
- Prevent back-to-back bookings without buffer
- Balance: fast turnaround = higher utilization, but need quality control

**Scenarios:**
- **Same-day turnaround:** Possible for quick returns, high demand
- **1-day buffer:** Standard for most rentals
- **2+ days:** Deep cleaning, repairs, or low demand periods

## Delivery & Collection Logistics

### Delivery Option

**Initial Phase: Founders Handle**
- Personal touch, build relationships
- Learn customer needs and pain points
- Gather feedback on experience
- Control quality of delivery experience

**Scaling Phase: Evaluate Hiring or 3rd Party**
- **Risk:** Losing customer touch if delivery person isn't from business
- **Options:** Hire dedicated delivery person (train on brand values) or use 3rd party (Grab, Lalamove)
- **Decision criteria:** Volume, cost, customer experience impact

**Delivery Fee Structure:**
- Top-up fee (keeps base rental price untouched)
- Some willing to pay for convenience
- Calculate: delivery cost vs. time spent on physical collection

**Premium Delivery Experience:**
- Professional packaging (unboxing experience)
- Onboarding materials included:
  - Cheat sheets with QR codes to video tutorials
  - Email/WhatsApp series (like Indiahikes' 11-email trek prep)
  - Self-guided setup instructions
- Maintain relationship even without physical presence

---

### Physical Collection Option

**Agreed Pickup Locations:**
- Convenient for both parties
- Safe, public locations
- Consistent locations for brand presence

**In-Person Experience:**
- Go through gear usage with customers
- Answer questions, provide tips
- Build personal relationships
- Immediate troubleshooting

**Benefits:**
- Stronger customer relationships
- Consultation opportunity for first-timers
- Build trust through face-to-face
- Gather direct feedback

**Time Investment:**
- Need to evaluate: time spent vs. value of relationship
- Some customers prefer physical collection (don't want to pay delivery fee)
- Balance: convenience vs. personal touch

---

### Future: Physical Location

**Vision:** Shoplot with good footfall, window shopping variety

**Benefits:**
- In-person experience at scale
- Community space for events
- "Decathlon for rentals" systematic process
- Customers constantly in/out
- Reduces delivery/collection coordination

**Timeline:** Long-term goal, not 5-year priority. Focus first on establishing KL/PJ presence.

## Peak Season Management

### Identifying Peak Periods

**Vacation-Focused Peaks:**
- School holidays
- Long weekends
- Public holidays
- Year-end travel season

**Lifestyle Segment:**
- More consistent year-round
- Event-driven (music festivals, weddings, etc.)

**Strategy:** Dual segment approach (adventure + lifestyle) smooths seasonal fluctuations.

---

### Handling High Demand

**Inventory Scaling:**
- Add inventory before peak seasons
- Backup equipment for overlapping bookings
- Prioritize high-demand items

**Pricing Strategy:**
- Dynamic pricing during peak times?
- Advance booking incentives (book early, pay less)
- Manage customer expectations when items unavailable

**Operational Capacity:**
- More frequent turnarounds
- Extended pickup/dropoff hours
- Additional staff if needed

---

### Handling Low Demand

**Utilization Strategies:**
- Off-peak promotions and discounts
- Target lifestyle segment (year-round needs)
- Corporate partnerships for team building events
- Content creation opportunities (use downtime for marketing)

**Maintenance Window:**
- Deep cleaning and repairs during slow periods
- Equipment upgrades and replacements
- System improvements and testing

---

## Pickup/Dropoff Slot Management

### Preventing Clashes

**Scheduling System:**
- Buffer times between slots
- Multiple time windows per day
- Flexible but clear boundaries

**Handling Delays and Late Returns:**
- Communication protocol with affected customers
- Backup equipment availability
- Grace period policy
- Late return fees (if necessary)

**Customer Communication:**
- Automated reminders before pickup/dropoff
- Clear instructions and location details
- Contact information for issues
- Proactive updates if delays occur

---

## Quality Control Measures

### Standardized Inspection Process

**Checklist for Each Item Type:**
- Visual inspection points
- Functionality tests
- Accessory verification
- Cleanliness standards

**Training Requirements:**
- How to assess damage vs. acceptable wear
- Consistent application of standards
- Documentation best practices
- Customer communication during disputes

**Documentation Standards:**
- Photo requirements (angles, lighting, detail)
- Video for high-value items
- Written notes on condition
- Customer sign-off process

---

### Handling Discrepancies

**When Customer and Inspector Disagree:**
1. Review documentation (photos/videos)
2. Compare to "acceptable wear" standards
3. Mediation and compromise approach
4. When to absorb costs vs. charge customers
5. Maintain relationship even in disagreement

**Goal:** Fair assessment while maintaining trust and customer relationship.

## Critical Operational Questions

1. **How fast do items need to turn around between renters?**
   - Define minimum based on inspection/cleaning needs
   - Balance utilization vs. quality control

2. **Who inspects gear, and how standardized is that process?**
   - Initially founders, need training materials for scaling
   - Create detailed checklists and visual guides

3. **What happens if pickup/dropoff slots clash?**
   - Buffer times, backup equipment, clear communication

4. **Is delivery economical, or does it break margins?**
   - Calculate costs, test with early customers
   - Determine when delivery makes sense vs. physical collection

5. **How do we maintain quality at scale?**
   - Standardized processes, training, quality control checks
   - Balance efficiency with customer experience
