# Unit Economics

## Break-Even Analysis

### Action Camera Example

**Purchase Cost:** RM 2,000 - 3,000 (average action camera)

**Market Rental Rate:**
- Premium latest models: RM 70-80/day
- Standard models: RM 60/day
- Top-ups: Larger SD card, extra batteries, etc.

**Break-Even Calculation:**
- Camera cost: RM 3,000
- Rental rate: RM 60/day
- **Break-even: ~50 rental days**

**Utilization Scenarios:**
| Utilization Rate | Days/Month | Months to Break-Even |
|------------------|------------|---------------------|
| High (50%) | 15 days | 3.3 months |
| Medium (30%) | 9 days | 5.5 months |
| Low (15%) | 4.5 days | 11 months |

### Technology Depreciation
- **New gear releases:** At least once a year

- **High-wear gear:** Action cameras, housings, mounts
- **Consumables:** Batteries, memory cards (shorter lifespan)

**Key Question:** When does gear need replacement vs. repair? How does this affect warranty or insurance?

## Deposit Structure

### Current Market: 50% of Item Value
**Example:** RM 3,000 camera = RM 1,500 deposit

**Problems:**
- Financially stressful for customers
- High barrier to entry
- Poor customer experience

---

### Our Approach: Lower Deposits (Insurance-Backed)

**Strategy:**
- Reasonable deposits (significantly less than 50%)
- Insurance covers gap between deposit and replacement cost
- Better customer experience, still protected

**Impact on Cash Flow:**
- Lower deposits = less working capital from customers
- Need sufficient operating capital to cover inventory
- Deposits shouldn't affect cash flow much (don't touch until rental over or item lost)

**Impact on Risk:**
- Lower deposit = higher risk if customer runs away
- Insurance mitigates this risk
- Need to calculate: insurance cost vs. risk exposure

**Alternative: Credit Card Authorization Holds**
- Hold maximum amount on credit card (like hotels, car rentals)
- Could eliminate deposits entirely for credit card holders
- **Problem:** Excludes customers without credit cards (potentially large segment in Malaysia)

**Need to Research:** Insurance viability, costs, coverage in Malaysia.

## Utilization Rate Impact

### Can This Work Without High Utilization?

**Factors:**
- Break-even utilization rate per item

- Lifestyle segment = year-round stability

- **Corporate partnerships:** Bulk rentals for events, team building

## Customer Lifetime Value (CLV)

### Rental Frequency Impact

| Segment | Frequency/Year | Annual Revenue/Customer | CLV (3 years) |
|---------|----------------|------------------------|---------------|
| Vacation renters | 1-3x | RM 180-540 (3 days @ RM 60) | RM 540-1,620 |
| Lifestyle documenters | 12x | RM 2,160 (3 days @ RM 60) | RM 6,480 |
| Serious hobbyists | 6x | RM 1,080 (3 days @ RM 60) | RM 3,240 |

**Key Insight:** Lifestyle segment has 4-12x higher CLV than vacation renters.

### Loyalty Program Economics

**Concept:** Rent enough times, get discount to buy

**Mechanics:**
- Track rental frequency per customer
- Offer discount on equipment purchase after X rentals
- Partner with retailers for referral fees

**Benefits:**
- Converts frequent renters before they buy elsewhere
- Generates affiliate revenue from retailer partnerships
- Maintains relationship even after they stop renting

**Question to Research:** What's the frequency threshold where buying becomes more economical than renting?

**Example Calculation:**
- Camera cost: RM 3,000
- Rental rate: RM 60/day x 3 days = RM 180/trip
- Break-even for customer: ~17 trips
- Loyalty trigger: Offer 20% discount (RM 2,400) after 12 rentals?
