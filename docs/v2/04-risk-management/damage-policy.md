# Damage Policy & Acceptable Wear

## Core Promise

### Wear-and-Tear Tolerance

**Our Guarantee:**
> "As long as wear and tear is minimal, no additional costs are incurred. We have good tolerance."

**Philosophy:**
- We recognize adventurers will push equipment to the max
- Some wear is expected and acceptable
- Remove fear of "getting penalized" for using equipment as intended
- Builds trust and alignment with audience's mindset

**Key Differentiator:** Adventure-aware policy, not punitive like traditional rentals.

---

## The Challenge

### What Is "Acceptable Wear"?

**Current Status:** Undefined and subjective

**Problems:**
- No clear definition of "minimal" or "reasonable"
- No visual standards or examples
- No process for consistent assessment
- Risk of customer disputes
- Inconsistent enforcement damages trust

**Need:** Clear, visual, documented standards that can be applied consistently.

---

## Defining Acceptable Wear (Need to Develop)

### Categories of Wear

#### 1. Acceptable Wear (No Charge)

**Cosmetic:**
- Minor scratches on housing exterior
- Light scuff marks from normal use
- Slight discoloration from sun/water exposure
- Worn straps or velcro (still functional)

**Functional:**
- Normal battery degradation
- Minor lens dust (doesn't affect image quality)
- Slight loosening of mounts (still secure)

**Adventure-Specific:**
- Sand in crevices (cleanable)
- Salt water residue (cleanable)
- Dirt and mud (cleanable)
- Minor wear from outdoor use

---

#### 2. Borderline Wear (Case-by-Case)

**Cosmetic:**
- Moderate scratches (visible but not deep)
- Dents that don't affect functionality
- Faded colors or markings

**Functional:**
- Sticky buttons (cleanable/fixable)
- Loose but functional mounts
- Minor water intrusion (dried, no damage)

**Decision Factors:**
- Does it affect next customer's experience?
- Is it repairable quickly and cheaply?
- Was it from reasonable use or negligence?
- Customer's rental history and relationship

---

#### 3. Unacceptable Damage (Customer Charged)

**Cosmetic (if affects functionality or next customer):**
- Deep scratches on lens (affects image quality)
- Cracked housing (compromises waterproofing)
- Broken clips or mounts (unusable)

**Functional:**
- Broken buttons or controls
- Water damage to electronics
- Cracked lens or screen
- Missing components
- Equipment doesn't power on or function

**Negligence:**
- Obvious misuse or abuse
- Failure to follow basic care instructions
- Damage from prohibited activities

## Assessment Process

### Inspection Protocol

**Before Rental (Outgoing):**
1. Photo/video documentation of equipment condition
2. Note any existing wear or marks
3. Show customer and get sign-off
4. Provide copy of condition report

**After Rental (Return):**
1. Photo/video documentation of returned condition
2. Compare to outgoing condition
3. Assess against "acceptable wear" standards
4. Discuss with customer if issues found
5. Document decision and reasoning

---

### Who Inspects?

**Initial Phase:** Founders

**Scaling Phase:** Trained staff

**Training Requirements:**
- Understand "acceptable wear" standards
- Consistent application of criteria
- Photo documentation best practices
- Customer communication skills
- Dispute resolution approach

**Quality Control:**
- Random audits of assessments
- Review disputed cases
- Update standards based on learnings
- Continuous training and calibration

## Preventing Abuse

### Red Flags

**Customer Behavior:**
- Excessive damage on first rental
- Pattern of damage across multiple rentals
- Evasive about how damage occurred
- Attempts to hide or not report damage
- Rushes return without inspection

**Damage Patterns:**
- Damage inconsistent with stated activity
- Multiple items damaged simultaneously
- Damage suggests misuse or prohibited activity
- Missing components without explanation

---

### Mitigation Strategies

**Customer Verification:**
- eKYC or identity verification
- Track customer history and patterns
- Start new customers with single kit
- Build trust over multiple rentals

**Clear Communication:**
- Provide care instructions upfront
- Explain acceptable wear policy clearly
- Show examples of what's okay vs. not okay
- Set expectations before rental

**Documentation:**
- Thorough before/after photos
- Customer sign-off on condition
- Clear evidence trail
- Consistent process every time

**Escalation:**
- When to refuse future service
- When to pursue legal recourse
- How to communicate decisions
- Balance protection with relationship

---

## Dispute Resolution

### When Customer Disagrees with Assessment

**Process:**
1. **Review Evidence**
   - Show before/after photos
   - Compare to "acceptable wear" standards
   - Explain reasoning clearly

2. **Listen to Customer**
   - Understand their perspective
   - Consider context (how damage occurred)
   - Assess if reasonable explanation

3. **Mediation**
   - Find middle ground if possible
   - Consider customer history and relationship
   - Weigh cost vs. relationship value

4. **Decision**
   - Apply standards consistently
   - Document reasoning
   - Communicate clearly and empathetically

5. **Maintain Relationship**
   - Even if charging, preserve trust
   - Explain it's about fairness, not punishment
   - Invite them to rent again

---

### When to Compromise

**Factors to Consider:**
- **Customer history:** First-time vs. repeat customer
- **Damage severity:** Minor vs. major cost
- **Explanation:** Reasonable accident vs. negligence
- **Relationship value:** CLV vs. cost of damage
- **Precedent:** Will this encourage abuse?

**Examples:**
- First-time customer, minor damage, good explanation → Absorb cost
- Repeat customer, major damage, no explanation → Charge full cost
- Borderline case, loyal customer → Split cost or absorb

**Goal:** Fair and consistent, but human and relationship-focused.

## Communication Strategy

### Setting Expectations Upfront

**During Booking:**
- Explain wear-and-tear tolerance policy
- Show examples of acceptable wear
- Provide care instructions
- Clarify what's covered vs. not covered

**In Confirmation:**
- Link to full policy and visual guide
- Remind of care instructions
- Encourage questions if unsure

**At Pickup:**
- Review condition together
- Point out any existing wear
- Remind of policy and care tips
- Encourage them to use gear fully

---

### Communicating Damage Charges

**Tone:**
- Empathetic and understanding
- Clear and factual (not accusatory)
- Fair and consistent
- Open to discussion

**Structure:**
1. Show evidence (photos)
2. Explain assessment (standards)
3. State charge (with reasoning)
4. Invite discussion
5. Maintain relationship

**Example:**
> "Hey [Name], thanks for returning the gear! During inspection, we noticed [specific damage] that goes beyond normal wear. Here are the before/after photos. Based on our standards, this requires [repair/replacement] which costs [amount]. We understand adventures can be unpredictable — let's chat if you have questions. We'd love to have you rent again!"

## Damage Cost Structure

### Repair Costs

**Minor Repairs:**
- Replacement straps, mounts: RM 20-50
- Button repairs: RM 50-100
- Cleaning and maintenance: No charge (included)

**Major Repairs:**
- Lens replacement: RM 200-500
- Housing replacement: RM 300-600
- Electronics repair: RM 500-1,000

**Downtime Cost:**
- Lost rental revenue while in repair
- Factor into damage charge or absorb?

---

### Replacement Costs

**Full Replacement:**
- Customer charged full replacement cost minus deposit
- Insurance covers if insured
- Self-insurance fund if not insured

**Partial Replacement:**
- Individual components (battery, mount, etc.)
- Charge actual replacement cost
- No markup (fair pricing)
