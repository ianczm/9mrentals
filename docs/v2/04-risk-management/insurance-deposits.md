# Insurance & Deposits Strategy

## Current Market Approach

### Standard Rental Deposits

**Typical Structure:**
- 50% of item's original price as deposit
- Example: RM 3,000 camera = RM 1,500 deposit

**Additional Requirements:**
- NRIC front and back copies
- Extensive legal documents to sign
- Payment information
- Some request payslips (to identify workplace location)

**Customer Impact:**
- Financially stressful
- Intimidating and invasive
- High barrier to entry
- Poor customer experience
- Customers understand why, but still dislike it

---

## Our Approach: Insurance-Backed Lower Deposits

### Strategy

**Core Concept:**
- Reasonable deposits (significantly less than 50%)
- Insurance covers gap between deposit and replacement cost
- Better customer experience while still protected

**Benefits:**
- Lower barrier to entry
- Less financially stressful for customers
- Faster onboarding (less intimidation)
- Professional and modern approach
- Competitive advantage

## Deposit Structure Options

### Option 1: Insurance-Backed Low Deposits

**Structure:**
- Deposit: 10-20% of item value (vs. 50% current market)
- Example: RM 3,000 camera = RM 300-600 deposit
- Insurance covers remaining risk

**Pros:**
- Lowest barrier to entry
- Best customer experience
- Competitive differentiation
- Professional approach

**Cons:**
- Requires insurance partnership
- Monthly premium costs
- May not be viable at small scale
- Claim process if loss occurs

---

### Option 2: Credit Card Authorization Holds

**Structure:**
- Hold full replacement value on credit card (like hotels, car rentals)
- No upfront deposit payment
- Charge only if damage/loss occurs

**Pros:**
- Zero deposit for customers
- Instant authorization
- Strong protection for business
- Proven model in other industries

**Cons:**
- Requires credit card (excludes customers without)
- Potentially large segment in Malaysia without credit cards
- Technical integration needed
- Customer needs sufficient credit limit

**Consideration:** Could offer as premium option alongside standard deposits.

---

### Option 3: Tiered Deposit System

**Structure:**
- **Standard:** 25-30% deposit (better than market's 50%)
- **Premium (with insurance add-on):** 10-15% deposit + insurance fee
- **Credit Card:** Authorization hold, no upfront deposit

**Pros:**
- Flexibility for different customer segments
- Still better than market even at standard tier
- Insurance as optional upgrade
- Inclusive (doesn't exclude non-credit card holders)

**Cons:**
- More complex to communicate
- Multiple pricing tiers
- Need to manage different risk levels

---

### Option 4: Self-Insurance Fund (Interim Solution)

**Structure:**
- Moderate deposits (25-30% of item value)
- Build self-insurance fund from profits
- Absorb losses from fund until scale justifies insurance

**Pros:**
- No insurance premiums initially
- Simpler to implement at launch
- Still better than market (50%)
- Path to insurance later

**Cons:**
- Higher risk exposure initially
- Need capital buffer for losses
- Less competitive than insurance-backed approach

---

## Cash Flow Implications

### Lower Deposits Impact

**Working Capital:**
- Lower deposits = less working capital from customers
- Need sufficient operating capital to cover inventory
- Can't rely on deposits to fund operations

**Cash Flow:**
- Deposits shouldn't affect cash flow much (don't touch until rental over or item lost)
- But lower deposits = less buffer if customer disappears
- Need capital reserves for replacement

**Risk Exposure:**
- Lower deposit = higher risk if customer runs away
- Insurance mitigates this risk
- Without insurance, need larger capital buffer

---

**Need to Calculate:**

## Risk Mitigation Beyond Deposits

### Customer Verification

**Minimum Necessary Data:**
- Contact information (phone, email)
- Identity verification (NRIC or alternative)
- Payment information
- Emergency contact (optional but recommended)

**Alternatives to NRIC Copies:**
- **eKYC (Electronic Know Your Customer):**
  - Digital identity verification services
  - Less invasive than physical copies
  - Instant verification
  - **Con:** Not exactly the best experience, may have friction

- **Credit Card Verification:**
  - Validates identity through financial institution
  - Authorization hold provides protection
  - **Con:** Excludes non-credit card holders

- **Social Media Verification:**
  - Real profiles with history (harder to fake)
  - Shows accountability
  - **Con:** Not legally enforceable

**Recommendation:** Custom onboarding survey for MVP. Evaluate eKYC post-MVP once there is time to properly assess Malaysian providers on UX and cost.

---

### Legal Recourse

**Reality Check:**
- Might not always be worth pursuing legal avenues even if we have them
- At best a scare tactic
- Cost and time of legal pursuit may exceed loss

**When to Pursue:**
- High-value losses (multiple items, expensive equipment)
- Clear evidence of theft (not just late return)
- Customer has assets worth pursuing
- Deterrent effect for other customers

**When to Write Off:**
- Low-value losses (cost of pursuit > loss)
- Ambiguous situations (lost vs. stolen)
- Customer genuinely unable to pay
- Relationship preservation more valuable

**Strategy:** Have legal recourse available, use selectively, communicate clearly as deterrent.

## Worst-Case Loss Scenarios

### Per-Item Loss

**Scenario:** Customer disappears with complete kit

**Loss:**
- Core item (e.g., action camera): RM 2,000-3,000
- Complete kit (housing, mounts, batteries, accessories): RM 500-1,000
- **Total loss:** RM 2,500-4,000 per kit

**Mitigation:**
- Deposit covers: RM 300-600 (20% deposit) or RM 750-1,200 (30% deposit)
- Insurance covers: Remaining RM 1,900-3,700 (if insured)
- Self-insurance fund: Absorb loss from reserves (if not insured)

---

### Multiple Simultaneous Losses

**Scenario:** Multiple customers disappear in short period (fraud pattern)

**Risk:**
- Could lose multiple kits before detecting pattern
- Significant capital impact
- May indicate systematic fraud

**Mitigation:**
- Customer verification (eKYC, credit card)
- Track patterns (new customers, similar profiles)
- Limit rentals to new customers (start with 1 kit)
- Insurance covers multiple losses
- Legal recourse for clear fraud cases

---

## Damage vs. Loss

### Damage (Repairable)

**Scenarios:**
- Cracked lens, broken mount, water damage (if repairable)
- Cosmetic damage beyond acceptable wear
- Functional issues requiring repair

**Cost:**
- Repair cost (varies by damage)
- Downtime (lost rental revenue)
- Replacement parts

**Coverage:**
- Deposit may cover repair costs
- Insurance may cover major damage
- Customer charged for damage beyond acceptable wear

---

### Loss (Irreparable or Missing)

**Scenarios:**
- Customer disappears with equipment
- Equipment destroyed beyond repair
- Lost during adventure (dropped in ocean, etc.)

**Cost:**
- Full replacement cost
- Lost rental revenue during replacement
- Administrative time

**Coverage:**
- Deposit provides partial coverage
- Insurance covers remaining (if insured)
- Legal recourse (if viable)

## Recommended Approach

### Phase 1: Launch (First 6-12 Months)

**Strategy:** Self-insurance with moderate deposits

**Structure:**
- 25-30% deposits (better than market's 50%)
- eKYC verification (less invasive than NRIC copies)
- Build self-insurance fund from profits
- Track loss rates and patterns

**Why:**
- Simpler to implement at launch
- No insurance partnership needed initially
- Still competitive vs. market
- Gather data for insurance negotiations

---

### Phase 2: Scale (After Proving Model)

**Strategy:** Insurance-backed low deposits

**Structure:**
- 10-20% deposits (insurance-backed)
- eKYC verification
- Insurance covers gap
- Optional credit card hold for zero deposit

**Why:**
- Best customer experience
- Strongest competitive advantage
- Scale justifies insurance costs
- Data from Phase 1 helps negotiate rates
