# MVP Launch Plan

## MVP Definition

**Minimum Viable Product:** Simplest version that delivers core value and validates business model.

**Core Features:**
- 3-5 action camera kits (complete with accessories)
- Basic website with real-time availability
- Self-service booking system
- Professional payment processing
- Flexible pickup/dropoff options
- Clear policies and terms

**Not Included in MVP:**
- Delivery (post-MVP)
- Physical location
- Large inventory variety
- Community events (yet)
- Advanced features
- Lifestyle gear (adventure only)

## Launch Strategy: First 100 Customers

### Phase 1: Friends & Family (First 10-20 Customers)

**Timeline:** Month 1

**Approach:**
- Personal invitations to close network
- Explain the vision and ask for support
- Offer special launch pricing or free rental
- Request detailed feedback
- Ask for testimonials and photos

**Goals:**
- Validate operations and customer experience
- Identify issues and pain points
- Generate initial testimonials and content
- Refine processes before wider launch
- Build confidence in service delivery

**Feedback Focus:**
- Booking process ease
- Equipment quality and completeness
- Pickup/dropoff experience
- Clarity of policies and terms
- Overall satisfaction
- What would make it better?

---

### Phase 2: Extended Network (Next 30-40 Customers)

**Timeline:** Months 2-3

**Approach:**
- Ask friends/family for referrals
- Post on personal social media
- Share in relevant Facebook groups
- Reach out to adventure communities
- Offer referral incentives

**Goals:**
- Expand beyond immediate network
- Test word-of-mouth engine
- Generate more testimonials and content
- Refine based on Phase 1 learnings
- Build initial customer base

**Marketing:**
- Share customer stories and photos
- Behind-the-scenes content
- Founder story and mission
- "If not now, when?" messaging
- Early adopter benefits

---

### Phase 3: Organic Growth (Next 30-40 Customers)

**Timeline:** Months 4-6

**Approach:**
- Consistent social media posting
- SEO and website traffic
- Partnerships with dive shops, tour operators
- Community engagement
- Small paid advertising budget (test)

**Goals:**
- Validate organic acquisition channels
- Test paid advertising effectiveness
- Build partnerships
- Establish brand presence
- Achieve product-market fit

**Metrics to Track:**
- Customer acquisition cost by channel
- Conversion rate (visitor to customer)
- Repeat rental rate
- Customer satisfaction (NPS)
- Referral rate

## MVP Success Criteria

### Operational Success:
- [ ] Smooth booking and rental process
- [ ] No major operational issues
- [ ] Equipment maintained and available
- [ ] Turnaround times manageable
- [ ] Customer verification working

### Customer Success:
- [ ] High customer satisfaction (NPS > 50)
- [ ] Positive testimonials and reviews
- [ ] Repeat customers (>20% repeat rate)
- [ ] Referrals happening organically
- [ ] No major complaints or disputes

### Financial Success:
- [ ] Pricing validated (customers willing to pay)
- [ ] Utilization rate acceptable (>15%)
- [ ] Break-even path clear
- [ ] Unit economics work
- [ ] Cash flow manageable

### Market Success:
- [ ] Product-market fit validated
- [ ] Target audience resonates with brand
- [ ] Word-of-mouth happening
- [ ] Differentiation clear vs. competitors
- [ ] Demand exists and growing

## Key Learnings to Validate

### Customer Behavior:
- Do they actually rent as frequently as expected?
- What activities and kits are most popular?
- What's the typical rental duration?
- Do they prefer delivery or physical collection?
- What questions and concerns come up most?

### Operations:
- How long does turnaround actually take?
- What damage and wear actually occurs?
- Are deposits sufficient?
- Is verification process smooth?
- What operational bottlenecks exist?

### Pricing:
- Is pricing competitive and profitable?
- Are customers price-sensitive?
- Do they pay for delivery?
- Are deposits acceptable?
- What's willingness to pay for premium?

### Marketing:
- Which channels drive customers?
- What messaging resonates?
- Does brand identity connect?
- What content performs best?
- How do customers find us?

## Pivot or Persevere Decision Points

### After First 10 Customers:

**Pivot If:**
- Major operational issues can't be resolved
- Customers don't see value
- Pricing doesn't work
- Feedback is consistently negative

**Persevere If:**
- Core value proposition validated
- Customers are satisfied
- Issues are fixable
- Demand exists

---

### After First 100 Customers:

**Pivot If:**
- Can't achieve product-market fit
- Unit economics don't work
- No organic growth or referrals
- Market is too small

**Persevere If:**
- Product-market fit achieved
- Customers love the service
- Word-of-mouth happening
- Path to profitability clear

## Risk Mitigation

### Risk: Low Demand

**Mitigation:**
- Start small (3-5 kits, low investment)
- Validate with friends/family first
- Test pricing and messaging
- Pivot quickly if needed

### Risk: Operational Issues

**Mitigation:**
- Simple processes initially
- Founders handle everything (learn deeply)
- Document and refine continuously
- Don't scale until smooth

### Risk: Equipment Damage/Loss

**Mitigation:**
- Start with trusted network
- Thorough verification process
- Clear policies and documentation
- Insurance or self-insurance fund

### Risk: Cash Flow

**Mitigation:**
- Sufficient capital buffer
- Don't over-invest in inventory initially
- Monitor cash flow closely
- Deposits help (but don't rely on them)
