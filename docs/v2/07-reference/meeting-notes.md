# Meeting Notes

## January 27, 2026 - Business Planning Session

**Attendees:** Ian, Nadree  
**Purpose:** Business planning session for adventure gear rental service

---

### Key Decisions & Insights

**Target Customer Profile:**
- Mid-20s to early 30s, Klang Valley, disposable income, tech-savvy
- Beginning to explore, high standards, want to document life-defining moments
- Three segments: Casual adventurers (50-60%), Content creators/lifestyle (25-35%), Serious hobbyists (10-20%)

**Brand Positioning:**
- Accommodate both beginners and pros
- Not luxury, but trusted quality with raw/adventurous branding
- Dual messaging: "Up your creativity game" (adventurers) and "Step out of comfort zone" (lifestyle)
- Core philosophy: "If not now, when?" — novelty, growth, never settling for comfort

**Differentiation:**
- Brand identity resonating with Klang Valley young adults
- Seamless UX with convenient pickup/dropoff
- Customer relationships (not mechanical like McDonald's)
- Insurance structure for low barrier to entry
- Loyalty deals and unique promotions

**Risk Management:**
- Worst case: customer disappears with complete kit
- Legal recourse might not always be worth pursuing (at best a scare tactic)
- Should keep documentation/pictures before and after renting
- Insurance might be viable for lower deposits and less legal friction

**Unit Economics:**
- Action cam: RM 2-3k, rental rate RM 60-80/day
- Break-even: ~50 rental days
- New gear releases yearly, rental prices drop annually
- Lower deposits = higher risk but better experience

**Operations:**
- Build custom inventory tracking system
- Delivery ideal but balance with personal relationships
- Top-up fee for delivery keeps base rental price untouched
- Need to consider inspection and maintenance

**Legal & Trust:**
- NRIC is market approach (scare tactic, can report to police)
- eKYC could be possible but not best experience
- Credit card authorization holds could work but excludes non-card holders
- Always document equipment condition before/after

**Brand Identity:**
- Inspiration: Red Bull (passion for adventure), Secret Life of Walter Mitty, Table & Apron/Bijou Cafe
- "If not now, when?" — pull trigger to step into new chapter
- "You will find 1000 ways to do something you don't want to do, 1000 ways to do something you want to"
- Underlying concept: novelty, growth, challenging yourself, never settling

**Long-Term Vision:**
- If rentals disappeared: still stand for enabling growth and stepping outside comfort zones
- Target market: people who want to keep outputting and growing
- Example: during COVID, creatives still wanted output → studio equipment rentals?
- As long as we tap into desire for growth, we'll figure out how to help

**Studio Rental Idea:**
- Studio with all equipment included
- People rent and "go absolutely ham"
- Charge clients real money, all because we enabled them
- No significant upfront capital needed for successful business

### Clarifications from Follow-Up Discussion

**Consultation Approach:**
- Consultation itself is not core business model — strong customer relationships are
- Priority: convenience and seamlessness first, then layer in relationships when feasible
- For first-timers: option to ask questions should be upfront, center, and effortless
- Goal: "wow, the people are so friendly, I want to keep renting from them"

**Delivery vs. Collection:**
- Both options available from start (customer chooses)
- Founders handle initially, evaluate hiring later (risk: losing customer touch)
- Delivery: top-up fee, premium experience with onboarding materials
- Physical collection: agreed pickup locations, go through gear usage

**Turnaround Time:**
- Customers select usage dates, we include additional buffer days for pickup/delivery/dropoff
- Clear boundaries and cutoff times (no negotiation, but flexible windows)
- Customers feel they're only paying for usage

**Strategic Priorities:**
- Start with adventure first, expand to lifestyle later
- Delivery and physical collection are equal options from start
- Community building: Year 1+ founders network, host events when customer base is decent

**Content Strategy:**
- Marketing tool (not revenue-generating product)
- Red Bull-inspired: UGC of people pushing limits (not just adventure, but all life moments)
- Customized branding campaigns to stand out
- Not boring testimonials — authentic stories of limit-pushing

**Geographic Expansion:**
- Not "phases" — these are long-term milestones
- 5-year realistic goal: Top rental service in KL/PJ
- Ambitious milestone: Rest of Malaysia (adventure tourism hotspots, not every state)
- Moonshot: SEA expansion with airport collection at destination

**5-Year Vision:**
- Realistic: Top rental service in KL/PJ with solid presence, strong customer base, potentially physical location
- Long-term dream (not 5-year): Physical shoplot "Decathlon for rentals", multiple brand arms
