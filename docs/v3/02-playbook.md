## What We're Building (MVP)

Adventure gear rental service. Phase 1: action camera kits only.

**MVP scope:**
- 2 kits at launch (Insta360 X5 + full accessories per kit)
- RM 10,000 capital (RM 5k per founder)
- Physical collection only — no delivery until post-MVP
- Website with real-time availability and self-service booking
- Custom onboarding survey for customer verification — no eKYC until post-MVP
- Stripe for payments (cards, FPX, Touch 'n Go, PayLater)

**Not in MVP:** delivery, lifestyle gear, loyalty program, paid ads, physical location, eKYC.

**Target launch:** March/April 2026

## Product

### Kit Configurations

**Diving Kit**
- Insta360 X5
- Dive housing (waterproof case)
- Invisible stick + mounts
- 2x extra batteries
- Memory card (sufficient capacity)
- Carry case

**Hiking Kit**
- Insta360 X5
- Chest mount + head mount
- 2x extra batteries
- Protective case

**Travel Vlog Kit**
- Insta360 X5
- Gimbal (post-MVP — add when lifestyle gear phase begins)
- 2x extra batteries
- Memory card

**Customizable (for pros):** à la carte selection from available components. Charge complexity fee or price individual items to incentivize pre-configured kits.

### Rental Period Structure

Customers select **usage dates** (what they pay for). We add buffer days around usage for pickup/dropoff logistics — customers feel they're only paying for actual usage time.

Example: 5-day diving trip (June 10–15) → pickup window June 8–9, dropoff window June 16–17. Customer pays for 5 days.

Clear cutoff times. No negotiation, but flexible windows agreed with customer upfront. Late fees part of terms and conditions.

## Pricing

**Promotions:**
- Book in advance, cheaper
- First customer, cheaper

**Deposit:** 25–30% of kit replacement value at launch (self-insured).
- Kit replacement value: ~RM 2,500–3,500 (confirm actual cost when purchasing)
- Deposit at 25%: ~RM 625–875 (need to think about it, use credit card to waive deposit)

**Payment:** Stripe. Customer pays rental fee + deposit at checkout. Deposit returned after inspection.

## Operations

### Booking Flow

1. Customer discovers via Instagram → visits website
2. Browses kits, checks real-time availability, selects dates
3. Registers + completes onboarding survey (3 parts: customer profile, risk/reliability, marketing fit)
4. Checkout — pays rental fee + 25% deposit via Stripe
5. Founders confirm via WhatsApp, agree on pickup location and time
6. Founders and customer meet, hand over gear, go through usage together
7. Customer goes on trip
8. Customer returns gear at agreed date/time
9. Visual inspection together (photos before and after — non-negotiable)
10. Deposit returned to customer
11. Founders take 1-day buffer for inspection, cleaning, QC before next rental

Customer can reach founders via WhatsApp at any point. Target response time: [define SOP].

## Customer Experience

What the customer should feel at each stage. This is as important as the operational flow above.

### Discovery & Browsing
**Goal:** Inspired and curious. Clear understanding of what we offer. Aligned with brand values. Wants to learn more.
- Website shows real-time availability, detailed kit contents, transparent pricing
- Testimonials and customer stories visible
- For first-timers: consultation option upfront and easy to find, not buried

### Booking & Payment
**Goal:** Easy and fast (minutes, not hours). Professional and secure. Clear next steps.
- Self-service, no back-and-forth
- Transparent pricing, no hidden fees
- Flexible pickup/dropoff window selection
- Reasonable deposit — not stressful

### Pre-Rental Communication
**Goal:** Prepared and informed. No last-minute surprises. Feels supported.
- Confirmation via WhatsApp with pickup details
- Reminder before pickup
- Contact info for questions clearly provided

### Pickup (Physical Collection — MVP)
**Goal:** Welcomed and supported. Confident they have everything. Excited for the adventure.
- Agreed location, convenient and safe
- Founders go through gear usage with customer in person
- Answer questions, provide tips
- Build personal relationship — this is a differentiator, not a chore

### During Rental
**Goal:** Empowered to capture amazing moments. No fear of damage. Support available if needed.
- Wear-and-tear tolerance explicitly communicated upfront — "use the gear fully, don't hold back"
- Founders reachable on WhatsApp if issues arise
- Reminder sent before dropoff window

### Return & Inspection
**Goal:** No rushing. Fair assessment. Satisfied with experience. Wants to rent again.
- Flexible dropoff window — time to transfer footage included
- Inspection done together (if physical), documented with photos
- Compare to outgoing condition transparently
- Deposit returned promptly if no issues
- Thank customer, invite them to share their experience

### Post-Rental
**Goal:** Feels part of something. Wants to share. Already thinking about next rental.
- Follow-up message: how was the experience?
- Invitation to share content (UGC for marketing)
- Referral incentive offered
- Community event invitations (when applicable)

### Service Standard
Not mechanical like a McDonald's kiosk. Authentic like a cafe with passion — Table & Apron, Bijou Cafe. Customers leave thinking "wow, the people are so friendly, I want to keep renting from them."

**Post-MVP additions:** Premium delivery experience (professional packaging, cheat sheets, QR codes to how-to videos, onboarding email series). Maintain relationship even without physical presence.

### Onboarding Survey (MVP Verification)

Three parts:
1. **Customer profile** — who they are, what they're doing, activity type
2. **Risk/reliability** — rental history, how they heard about us, emergency contact
3. **Marketing fit** — "our people" rating, how they found us, content sharing consent

Survey serves dual purpose: risk screening AND marketing segmentation. Frame it as onboarding, not gatekeeping. Consider: "Get verified and receive RM 20 off your first RM 100+ rental" to reduce friction.

### Turnaround Protocol

Between every rental:
1. Return inspection + photo documentation
2. Compare to outgoing condition
3. Assess against acceptable wear standards
4. Clean and sanitize
5. Test all functionality
6. Charge batteries, format memory cards
7. Repack kit
8. Mark available in inventory system

Standard buffer: 1 day between rentals. Deep clean or repair: 2+ days. Remove from inventory if repair needed.

### Inspection & Documentation

**Before every rental (outgoing):**
- Photo/video of full kit condition
- Checklist: all components present, functionality tested, batteries charged, cards formatted
- Customer signs off on condition report

**After every rental (return):**
- Photo/video of returned condition
- Compare to outgoing photos
- Assess damage against acceptable wear standards
- Document decision and reasoning

Photos are non-negotiable. Every rental, every time.

## Damage Policy

### Core Promise

> "As long as wear and tear is minimal, no additional costs. We have good tolerance."

Adventurers push gear. Some wear is expected and acceptable. Remove the fear of getting penalized for using equipment as intended.

### Acceptable Wear (No Charge)

- Minor scratches on housing exterior
- Light scuff marks from normal use
- Slight discoloration from sun/water exposure
- Sand in crevices (cleanable), salt water residue (cleanable), dirt and mud (cleanable)
- Normal battery degradation
- Minor lens dust (doesn't affect image quality)

### Borderline (Case-by-Case)

- Moderate scratches (visible but not deep)
- Dents that don't affect functionality
- Sticky buttons (cleanable/fixable)
- Minor water intrusion (dried, no damage)

Decision factors: Does it affect next customer's experience? Is it repairable quickly and cheaply? Was it reasonable use or negligence? Customer's rental history.

### Unacceptable Damage (Customer Charged)

- Deep scratches on lens (affects image quality)
- Cracked housing (compromises waterproofing)
- Broken buttons or controls
- Water damage to electronics
- Cracked lens or screen
- Missing components
- Equipment doesn't power on

**Action item:** Create visual guide with photo examples of each category before first rental. This is non-negotiable — disputes without visual standards are unwinnable.

### Dispute Resolution

1. Show before/after photos
2. Compare to visual standards
3. Listen to customer's explanation
4. Find middle ground if reasonable
5. Document decision
6. Maintain relationship — even if charging, preserve trust

When to absorb: first-time customer, minor damage, good explanation, relationship value > cost.
When to charge: repeat damage pattern, negligence, major cost, no reasonable explanation.

## Risk & Deposits

### Self-Insurance (Phase 1)

At launch, we self-insure. Deposits (25–30%) provide partial coverage. Build a self-insurance fund from profits to absorb losses.

Worst-case per kit: RM 2,500–4,000 (camera + full accessories). Deposit covers RM 625–1,050. Remaining exposure: RM 1,500–3,000 per lost kit.

Mitigation: start with trusted network, thorough verification, clear documentation, legal recourse as deterrent (not primary strategy).

### Insurance (Phase 2 Target)

Once insurance partner is secured, reduce deposits to 10–20%. This is a key competitive differentiator — research is a pre-launch priority.

**Action item:** Get quotes from Malaysian equipment rental insurance providers. Understand coverage (theft, damage, adventure activities), exclusions, premiums, and minimum scale requirements.

### Legal Recourse

Available but not primary strategy. Cost of pursuit often exceeds loss. Use as deterrent — communicate clearly in T&Cs. Pursue only for high-value losses with clear evidence.

**MVP:** Custom onboarding survey (see above). No eKYC until post-MVP.

**Post-MVP:** Evaluate eKYC providers in Malaysia on UX and cost. Some providers are buggy — don't implement until properly assessed. Credit card authorization hold is also worth evaluating (proven model: hotels, car rentals; risk: excludes non-card holders).

**Data we collect:** Contact info, identity (NRIC via survey), payment info, emergency contact. PDPA-compliant — get legal review before launch.

## Launch Strategy: First 100 Customers

**Phase 1 — Friends & Family (Month 1, 10–20 customers)**
Personal invitations. Explain the vision, ask for support. Offer special launch pricing or free rental. Gather detailed feedback on everything. Request testimonials and photos.

**Phase 2 — Extended Network (Months 2–3, 30–40 customers)**
Ask for referrals. Post on personal social media. Share in relevant Facebook groups and adventure communities. Offer referral incentives.

**Phase 3 — Organic Growth (Months 4–6, 30–40 customers)**
Consistent social media. SEO. Partnerships with dive shops, tour operators. Small paid advertising test.

**Success criteria at 100 customers:**
- NPS > 50
- >20% repeat rental rate
- No major operational issues
- Unit economics validated
- Word-of-mouth happening organically
