# Investor Gaps & Weak Justifications

> Analysis of claims across v3 docs that lack supporting data, are poorly justified, or are overly ambitious without a concrete plan.
> Use this as a pre-pitch checklist — resolve these before presenting to investors or partners.

---

## Market & Demand Claims

### "70% of Kuari Pass hikers are mid-20s to early 30s"
**Files:** `03-vision.md`, `07-growth.md`
**Problem:** Kuari Pass is a single trail in Pahang. It's not representative of Klang Valley's adventure market — it's a small, specific subset of our target audience. An investor will immediately ask: what's the source, and why does one trail's demographic validate a KL-based rental business?
**Fix:** Replace with broader data — MDTCA tourism statistics, adventure activity participation rates in KL/PJ, or primary research from a survey of the target segment.

---

### "Revenge travel drove a surge in experiential spending"
**Files:** `03-vision.md`
**Problem:** Cited as "Malaysia Travel Trends 2022" — that's 4 years old. The revenge travel wave has largely normalized. Using it as a demand driver feels dated.
**Fix:** Either find current data (2024–2025) or reframe this as a structural behavioral shift rather than a post-pandemic spike.

---

### "Solo travel is rising among young Malaysians"
**Files:** `07-growth.md`
**Problem:** Cited as "Business Today Malaysia, 2025" with no article title, no link, no specific stat. Unverifiable as written.
**Fix:** Find the actual article, pull the specific stat, and cite it properly. Or remove it if it can't be sourced.

---

### Customer segment percentages (50–60% casual, 25–35% creators, 10–20% hobbyists)
**Files:** `01-pitch.md`
**Problem:** These numbers appear with no basis — no research, no survey, no logic explained. Reads like made-up precision.
**Fix:** Either source these from market research, or explicitly label them as estimates and explain the reasoning behind the split.

## Unit Economics

### 30% utilization as "base case"
**Files:** `01-pitch.md`
**Problem:** 30% utilization (~9 days/month) is presented as a base case with no justification. For a brand-new business with 2 kits and no customer base, this is optimistic. Month 1 could realistically be 5–10%.
**Fix:** Model three scenarios — conservative (10%), base (20%), optimistic (30%). Show the ramp over 6–12 months, not just a steady-state assumption.

---

### Kit cost "~RM 2,500–3,500"
**Files:** `01-pitch.md`, `02-playbook.md`
**Problem:** Flagged as unvalidated in `decisions.md` (D08). Insta360 X5 body alone is ~RM 2,000+. Full dive kit with housing, batteries, mounts, and accessories could be RM 4,000–5,500+. If kit cost is wrong, deposit calculation is wrong, break-even is wrong, and the RM 10k capital claim becomes questionable.
**Fix:** Price out the full kit before any pitch. This is the single most important number to validate.

---

### RM 10k capital vs. RM 23,500–41,000 full MVP budget
**Files:** `01-pitch.md`, `04-decisions.md` (D09)
**Problem:** There's a RM 13,500–31,000 gap between committed capital and estimated budget with no concrete plan to bridge it. "Phase the spend" is mentioned but never elaborated.
**Fix:** Build a phased budget — what does RM 10k actually cover, what gets deferred, and what's the trigger for additional spend? If external capital is needed, say so explicitly.

## Validation Gap

### Customer interviews not yet done
**Files:** `01-pitch.md`, `02-playbook.md`
**Problem:** "10–20 customer interviews" is listed as a pre-launch action item — meaning demand is assumed, not validated. The docs present customer pain points and willingness to pay as established facts when they're actually hypotheses.
**Fix:** Do the interviews before pitching. Even 5–10 conversations with target customers changes the narrative from "we think" to "customers told us." This is the highest-leverage thing to do before any pitch.

## Growth & Acquisition

### CAC targets are qualitative, not quantitative
**Files:** `07-growth.md`
**Problem:** The CAC table uses "Low", "Very low", "Medium-High" — no numbers. Without actual estimates, the "CAC < 1/3 of CLV" target is meaningless.
**Fix:** Estimate rough CAC per channel even if directional — e.g., referral cost = RM 20–40 incentive, paid social = RM 50–150/customer based on Malaysian benchmarks. Refine with actual data once live.

---

### "First 1,000 customers in months 6–18"
**Files:** `07-growth.md`
**Problem:** No acquisition math behind this milestone. No conversion rates, no referral assumptions, no channel breakdown.
**Fix:** Work backwards — if average customer rents 2x/year, 1,000 customers = 2,000 rentals/year. At 2 kits and ~30% utilization, max capacity is ~216 rental-days/year. You'd need significantly more inventory before hitting 1,000 customers. The milestone needs to be tied to inventory scaling.

---

### Paid advertising budget unspecified
**Files:** `07-growth.md`
**Problem:** "Small test budget" is mentioned but never sized. For a business with RM 10k total capital, any paid spend needs to be planned.
**Fix:** Define a specific test budget (e.g., RM 500–1,000 for first paid campaign) and what success looks like before scaling.

---

## Insurance

### Insurance completely unresearched
**Files:** `02-playbook.md`, `04-decisions.md`
**Problem:** Insurance is listed as a "pre-launch priority" but no quotes have been obtained. The Phase 2 deposit reduction (10–20%) is entirely contingent on finding an insurance partner. If insurance is unavailable, prohibitively expensive, or excludes adventure activities, a key differentiator disappears.
**Fix:** Get at least 2–3 quotes from Malaysian equipment rental insurance providers before pitching. Understand coverage scope, exclusions (adventure activities are often excluded), premiums, and minimum scale requirements. RentSmart Asia's RM 10,000/item coverage is a useful benchmark.

## Vision Claims

### Red Bull comparison
**Files:** `03-vision.md`
**Problem:** Red Bull spent hundreds of millions over decades. Referencing it as a direct model without qualification can read as naive in a pitch context.
**Fix:** Ground it: "we're building the adventure community brand for KL — the way Red Bull built for extreme sports globally — starting local, staying authentic, brand-first from day one."

---

### "Rent in KL, collect in Bali or Bangkok" moonshot
**Files:** `03-vision.md`
**Problem:** Cross-border logistics, customs, insurance across multiple jurisdictions — mentioned with zero acknowledgment of complexity. An investor reading this might question judgment.
**Fix:** Either remove it or explicitly frame it as a 10+ year aspiration contingent on solving significant operational, legal, and insurance challenges across SEA markets.
